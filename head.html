The Great Himalayas, also known as the Greater Himalayas or Himadri, form the highest mountain range in the world. Stretching 2,400 km (1,500 miles) from west to east, they separate the Indian subcontinent’s plains from the Tibetan Plateau. Within this majestic range, peaks like Mount Everest, Kangchenjunga, Lhotse, and Nanga Parbat rise to awe-inspiring heights. Glaciers, including the Gangotri and Satopanth, are nestled within these ancient peaks.
The holy river Ganga originating in the western Himalayas in Uttarakhand, it journeys 2,525 km (1,569 mi) south and east through the Gangetic plain. In Hinduism, the Ganga is considered the holiest river. It is personified as the goddess Ganga and revered for its ability to cleanse sins and grant Moksha (liberation from the cycle of birth and death). Bathing in its waters is believed to purify the soul, and the ashes of the deceased are immersed in its sacred flow
The Great Himalayan National Park is a treasure trove of medicinal plants. These plants serve both as a blessing and a dilemma for the park’s floral biodiversity. During the rainy season, the alpine meadows come alive with colorful flowers, including many herbs and shrubs prized for their medicinal properties.
